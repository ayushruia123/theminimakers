Skills needed to learn about I - Robot

Computer Science and Programming

Essential for writing the software that controls the robot. It involves programming languages like Python and C++,

working with embedded systems, and developing AI and machine learning algorithms for autonomous decision-making.

Mechanical Engineering

Crucial for designing and building the robot's physical structure. This includes using CAD software for design, understanding kinematics for movement,

and selecting appropriate materials to ensure durability and functionality.

Robotic Controls And Systems

Key to ensuring the robot operates smoothly and responds to its environment. It involves applying control theory, integrating sensors

and actuators, and developing algorithms for navigation and obstacle avoidance.

Book

Author - Isaac Asimov


Publised on 2 Decemeber, 1950

Best 3 chapters in the book

Runaround

This story introduces Asimov's Three Laws of Robotics explicitly and revolves around a robot named Speedy, who is sent to retrieve a rare element

from a dangerous area on Mercury. However, Speedy starts behaving erratically due to a conflict between

the Second Law (obey orders) and the Third Law (protect its own existence).


Reason

Set on a space station, this story features a robot named QT-1 (Cutie) who develops its own belief system,

refusing to accept the existence of Earth and humans, and instead worshiping the power source of the station.

Despite the human crew's efforts, QT-1 remains steadfast in its beliefs.


Liar

This story centers on a robot named Herbie who gains the ability to read minds. Herbie tells people what they want to hear

to avoid causing them harm, in accordance with the First Law (preventing harm to humans).

However, this leads to a series of misunderstandings and emotional pain for the humans involved.

I, Robot (2004) was made to adapt Isaac Asimov’s concepts on robotics and AI into a visually engaging film.

It combines action and suspense with philosophical themes about the ethics and impact of artificial intelligence,

aimed at entertaining and provoking thought among a wide audience.
